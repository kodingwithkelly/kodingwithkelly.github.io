About me

Hi, I'm Kelly.

I am an aspiring data analyst with a passion for data and extracting meaningful insights to create visualizations from complex problems.

I graduated Cum Laude from San Jose State Univeristy in Fall 2020 with a focus in Applied and Computational Mathematics and a minor in Economics. By studying computational math at SJSU, I was able to rediscover my interest in analytics. My interest for analyzing data and story telling with data stems from fond memories of when I was in high school helping my immigrant dad proofread presentations for work.

Since graduating I have dedicated my time to learning and improving upon my techincal skills by reading books like "Making Sense of Data" by Glenn J. Myatt and "Storytelling with Data" by Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic; taking MOOCs on Udemy and Coursera, and applying my skills to personal projects.

Aside from that, I enjoy speed solving Rubik's cubes and weight training in my spare time.

    Spatial Analysis on Homelessness in the Bay Area

    (ArcGIS, SQL, Excel)

    A report that maps and analyzes homelessness in Alameda County and the city's responses to homelessness in 2015-2019. Indicators for analysis include median household income, median gross rent, and a calculated gini coefficient. Percentages of racial/ethnic groups were also represented in chloropeth maps with data showing a 46% increase in homelessness and 49.64% reduction of shelter resources.
